Description

Complete Blood Count (CBC) is a blood test that evaluates your overall health and detects a wide range of disorders including anemia, infection, and leukemia. It measures several components of your blood including red blood cells, white blood cells, hemoglobin, and platelets.

This test is often used as a screening test during routine medical examinations and can help detect various diseases that affect blood cells.

  • If you have signs of infection

  • Before surgery or medical procedures

Description

The Vitamin D & B12 Combo test measures the levels of two essential vitamins in your body. Vitamin D is crucial for bone health and immune function, while Vitamin B12 is important for nerve function, red blood cell formation, and energy production.

This combo test is ideal for individuals experiencing fatigue, weakness, or those who may be at risk of deficiency due to dietary restrictions, age, or certain medical conditions.

  • If you follow a vegetarian or vegan diet (B12 deficiency risk)
  • If you have limited sun exposure (Vitamin D deficiency risk)
  • For adults over 50 years of age
  • If you have digestive disorders affecting nutrient absorption

The Thyroid Profile Total test evaluates how well your thyroid gland is functioning by measuring the levels of thyroid hormones in your blood. The thyroid gland regulates metabolism, energy production, and affects various body functions.

This comprehensive profile helps diagnose thyroid disorders such as hypothyroidism (underactive thyroid) or hyperthyroidism (overactive thyroid), which can affect growth, development, and many body functions.

Description

The Diabetes Screening test measures your blood glucose levels to help identify diabetes or prediabetes. This test includes fasting blood glucose, postprandial glucose, and HbA1c which provides information about your average blood glucose levels over the past 2-3 months.

Early detection of diabetes is crucial for preventing complications and managing the condition effectively. This screening is especially important for individuals with risk factors such as family history, obesity, or sedentary lifestyle.

Parameters Included (3)

Fasting Blood Glucose
Post Prandial Blood Glucose
HbA1c (Glycated Hemoglobin)

When to Take This Test

  • As part of an annual health checkup, especially after age 45
  • If you have symptoms of diabetes (frequent urination, increased thirst)
  • If you have a family history of diabetes
  • If you are overweight or obese
  • If you have high blood pressure or high cholesterol
  • For women with a history of gestational diabetes
